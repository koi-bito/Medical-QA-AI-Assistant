## Day 2

Why are embeddings useful? What does "similar meaning = similar vector" actually mean?
- **An embedding is a list of numbers (a vector) that represents a word, sentence, or piece of text in a multi-dimensional space.Computers can only work with numbers, not words. Embeddings solve this by converting text into a vector — an ordered list of numbers — that captures the meaning of that text.When two words or sentences have similar meanings, their vectors end up close together in vector space. For example: "happy" and "joyful" will have vectors that are nearly pointing in the same direction, while "happy" and "car" will be far apart.**
